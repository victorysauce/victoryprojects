A modern-day flâneuse with a camera in hand, Anastasia Samoylova observes the everyday to reveal its absurdities and its quietly accumulating violence.

Anastasia Samoylova is one of the most distinctive voices in contemporary American photography. Adaptation is the first major survey of her work, presenting five of her most significant series: Floridas, FloodZone, Landscape Sublime, Image Cities, and Breakfasts.

As a first-generation immigrant to the United States, Samoylova sees both the surface America presents and what lies beneath it. Wandering through urban and 'natural' landscapes, her meticulously layered images invite the viewer to decode and interpret the word "adaptation" across themes such as capitalism, climate change, gentrification, and the gendered occupation of public space.

Intertwining aesthetics with socio-politics, her work responds to our rapidly changing world with curiosity, humor, and pointed critique.

In its first showing at Saatchi Gallery, London, the exhibition drew more than 40,000 visitors. It is now available to travel.

Key Themes
Climate & Environment
FloodZone documents the American South where resort architectures and manicured coastlines, built in defiance of nature, are slowly being reclaimed by it. The kaleidoscopic collages of Landscape Sublime challenge our habit of treating landscape as something that exists to be looked at, consumed, or framed.
Capitalism & Urban Space
With Floridas, Samoylova renders Florida as a microcosm of the United States — a layered socio-political landscape of nostalgia, economic decay, and widening inequality. Image Cities continues this exploration across the capitals of Europe and Asia, tracking the ways visual capitalism and the gendered gaze shape how we see and move through cities.
The Photographic Canon
The Breakfasts series stages playful, witty, tongue-in-cheek conversations with the history of photography, its canonical images reduced to props on a breakfast table. In the age of the "lifestyle influencer," Samoylova's careful, humorous appropriations ask who gets to be taken seriously, and why.
Curator
Taous Dahmani

Taous Dahmani is a London-based art historian specialising in photography. Since September 2025, she is Curator at the Photographers' Gallery, London. Her curatorial projects include the 2022 Louis Roederer Discovery Award at Les Rencontres d'Arles, the 2024 Jaou Photo Biennale in Tunis, and solo exhibitions at NOUA (Norway), Saatchi Gallery (London), and Kyotographie (Japan). She is associate editor of Shining Lights: Black Women Photographers in 1980s–90s Britain (MACK/Autograph ABP, 2024), and has published with Phaidon, Tate, Pompidou, Aperture, and FOAM.

New institutions may commission their own curatorial text. Dahmani is available for catalogue contributions and public programming.
